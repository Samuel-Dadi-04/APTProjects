ABOUT US

Andhra Polytechnic College, Kakinada is one of the oldest polytechnic colleges in the state of Andhra Pradesh, India. The college was established in the year 1946 by the East Godavari District Board, with the aim of providing technical education to the youth of the region.

The college initially started with three diploma courses in Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, and Civil Engineering. In the year 1950, the college added a diploma course in Chemical Engineering, making it the first polytechnic college in the state to offer this course.

HISTORY OF APT

Over the years, Andhra Polytechnic College has grown to become one of the premier technical institutions in the region, offering diploma courses in a variety of engineering disciplines, including Electronics and Communication Engineering, Computer Engineering, and Automobile Engineering.

The college has produced thousands of skilled technicians and engineers who have gone on to make significant contributions to various industries in India and abroad. The college has also been recognized by various professional bodies, including the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) and the State Board of Technical Education and Training (SBTET).
